# 🍽️ Zomato Bangalore Restaurant Analysis

A dual-tool data analytics project exploring restaurant trends across Bangalore using Excel and Power BI — built on the Zomato Bangalore Dataset (Kaggle, 7,086 restaurants).

---

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes the Zomato Bangalore restaurant dataset to uncover business insights around customer ratings, cuisine popularity, location trends, online ordering behavior, and pricing patterns. The same dataset was analyzed using two industry-standard tools to demonstrate proficiency in both.

---

## 📊 Dataset
**Source:** [Zomato Bangalore Dataset — Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/abhijitdahatonde/zomato-restaurants-dataset)  
**Scope:** 7,086 restaurants across Bangalore  
**Key Fields:** Restaurant name, Address, cuisine type, average cost for two, online order availability, table booking availability, customer ratings, restaurant type  

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

### 01 — Digital Presence
- Restaurants accepting online orders average a rating of **3.55 vs 3.47** for offline-only (+0.08 gap)  
- This pattern holds consistently across all price tiers  

### 02 — Premium Segment
- Restaurants offering table booking score **4.04 avg vs 3.45** without — a +0.58 gap  
- Only **10.5%** of restaurants offer table booking → heavily underserved premium segment  

### 03 — Restaurant Type
- Quick Bites dominate volume (**40% — 2,840 restaurants**) but average only **3.40 rating**  
- Pub/Microbrewery types achieve the highest avg rating at **4.44** despite being a niche  

### 04 — Cuisine Analysis
- North Indian is the most common cuisine (**1,942 restaurants**)  
- Continental leads quality with the highest avg rating of **3.81**  
- Biryani has the lowest avg rating (**3.35**) despite being the 5th most common  

### 05 — Location Analysis
- Whitefield (**459**) and BTM (**458**) lead in restaurant count  
- Lavelle Road leads in quality with an avg rating of **4.06** (only 38 restaurants)  

### 06 — Rating Distribution
- **67%** of Bangalore restaurants are rated between **3.0–3.9**  
- Only **1.4%** achieve ratings above **4.5** — true excellence is rare  
- **15.2%** of restaurants fall below **3.0**

## 💡 Business Recommendations
- Invest in digital ordering — online restaurants consistently rate higher across all price tiers  
- Target the premium segment — table booking is underserved (only 10.5%) yet correlates with +0.58 rating advantage  
- Quick Bites need quality focus — high volume but stuck below 3.5; food consistency is the lever  
- Explore high-rating niches — Continental, Microbreweries, and Casual Dining Bars outperform despite lower saturation  
- Expansion targets — Whitefield, BTM, Electronic City, Marathahalli show high density and growth potential  
- Quality differentiation is rare — only 17% of restaurants rate above 4.0; genuine quality investment stands out
